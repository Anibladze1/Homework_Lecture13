1. Author: J.K. Rowling
    Title: Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone
    Description: Harry Potter is a young orphan who discovers that he is a wizard and is whisked off to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Along the way, he makes friends with Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger and faces off against the dark wizard, Lord Voldemort, who killed his parents.
  2. Author: George Orwell
    Title: 1984
    Description: In a dystopian future, the government controls every aspect of citizens' lives and manipulates the truth to maintain power. The protagonist, Winston, begins to secretly rebel against the government, but ultimately discovers the true cost of freedom.
  3. Author: Jane Austen
    Title: Pride and Prejudice
    Description: Set in early 19th-century England, Pride and Prejudice tells the story of the Bennett family and their relationships with the wealthy, eligible bachelors in their social circle. The novel follows the romance between Elizabeth Bennett and Mr. Darcy, as they overcome societal expectations and their own pride and prejudices.
  4. Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald
    Title: The Great Gatsby
    Description: Set in the summer of 1922, The Great Gatsby tells the story of the mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby and his relationship with the beautiful and troubled Daisy Buchanan. The novel explores themes of love, obsession, and the corrupting influence of wealth.
  5. Author: Margaret Atwood
    Title: The Handmaid's Tale
    Description: In a dystopian future where the United States has been replaced by a theocratic regime, women's rights have been severely curtailed. The protagonist, Offred, is a handmaid, whose sole purpose is to bear children for wealthy couples. As she navigates the dangerous world of the Commander and his wife, Offred begins to uncover the disturbing secrets of her society.
